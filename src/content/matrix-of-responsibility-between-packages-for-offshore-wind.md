# Matrix of Responsibility Between Packages for Offshore Wind

*By techmadeeasy team*

Think of the offshore wind farm project as a huge puzzle. It's so big that it's tough for just one company to put it all together. That's why the project owner will probably split it into smaller pieces, called "packages," and manage them separately. Making sure all these pieces fit together perfectly is super important.

Now, before any construction starts, there's a tricky part called the "responsibility matrix." It's like a game plan that shows who does what. We're going to take a closer look at the usual packages and the tools needed to make this game plan for our wind farm project.

---

## The Key Packages

### Wind Turbine Group (WTG)

This team takes care of everything about the wind turbines — from designing, supply and testing to making sure they work. The installation might be handled by another package (for example T&I, see below).

### Offshore Substation

If we need a substation in the sea to step up the voltage for transferring power over longer distances, there's a team for that too. They design it, build it, and make sure it has a strong foundation and good equipment in place.

### WTG Foundations

A specialized team with large fabrication yards manufactures the huge steel bases for the wind turbines — like jackets and monopiles — and ships them out to site.

### Subsea Cables

Only a few companies in the world manufacture the underwater cables we need, so they're dedicated to this scope.

### Onshore Substation and Lines

A local team designs the power station on land and connects it to the power grid, following all the local rules and regulations.

### Transport & Installation (T&I) and Balance of Plant (BOP)

This crew collects all the big parts from the other teams and uses massive vessels to put everything in place at sea.

### Employer (The Project Boss)

The company that owns the wind farm watches over everything to make sure all the teams work together smoothly.

---

## The RACI Matrix

To avoid any mix-ups about who does what, we use a **RACI matrix**. It's like a chart that helps everyone understand their job:

- **R** — Responsible (does the work)
- **A** — Accountable (owns the outcome)
- **S** — Support (helps with the work)
- **C** — Consult (provides input before decisions)
- **I** — Inform (kept in the loop after decisions)
- **V** — Validate (checks and approves the output)

---

## Work Categories

We also break down the work into categories to make the matrix actionable:

- **Design** — Engineering and detailed design deliverables
- **Supply** — Procurement and manufacturing of components
- **Loading & Transport** — Moving components from factory to port to site
- **Installation** — Offshore and onshore construction activities
- **Testing & Commissioning** — Ensuring everything works before handover

It's super important that everyone knows their role to plan the project well. A clear responsibility matrix prevents gaps, overlaps, and finger-pointing when things get complex.
